SSC CHSL 2026 — Combined Higher Secondary Level Recruitment

Verified guide — eligibility, selection process, and official apply link. Always confirm dates on the government portal before applying.
Category: Central Govt Jobs Updated: June 2026

📅 Last updated: June 18, 2026

SSC Combined Higher Secondary Level (CHSL) examination recruits candidates who have passed 12th standard into central government clerical and data entry posts. SSC CHSL 2026 offers positions such as Lower Division Clerk (LDC), Data Entry Operator (DEO), Postal Assistant (PA) and Sorting Assistant (SA) in various ministries and departments. It is among the most popular recruitment exams for intermediate-qualified youth seeking stable government employment.

Eligibility

  • Nationality: Indian citizen or eligible categories as per SSC rules.
  • Educational Qualification: 12th pass (Higher Secondary) or equivalent from a recognized board or university.
  • For DEO Grade A in C&AG: 12th pass in Science stream with Mathematics as a subject, OR 12th pass with Mathematics at 10th level.
  • Age Limit: 18–27 years. Relaxation: OBC — 3 years; SC/ST — 5 years; PwBD — 10 years.
  • Skill Requirements: English/Hindi typing speed required for LDC and DEO posts (tested in Tier 2 skill test).

Advertisement

Selection Process

  • Tier 1: CBT — General Intelligence, General Awareness, Quantitative Aptitude and English Language (200 marks, 60 minutes).
  • Tier 2: CBT — Mathematical Abilities, Reasoning & General Intelligence, English Language & Comprehension and General Awareness (each section timed separately).
  • Tier 3: Skill Test / Typing Test — Typing speed of 35 wpm in English or 30 wpm in Hindi for LDC; 8,000 key depressions per hour for DEO.
  • Document Verification: Shortlisted candidates must produce original certificates for age, education, category and other claims.

How to Apply

  1. Open ssc.gov.in and locate the SSC CHSL 2026 notification under the 'Notice' or 'Apply' section.
  2. Complete One-Time Registration (OTR) if not already registered on the SSC portal.
  3. Select the CHSL examination, fill all mandatory fields and upload scanned photo and signature.
  4. Pay the prescribed fee through net banking, debit/credit card or UPI.
  5. Review the application carefully before final submission and save the registration slip.
  6. Download admit card from SSC portal when released for Tier 1 examination.
