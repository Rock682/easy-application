AP DSC 2026 — Teacher Recruitment (TRT)

Verified guide — eligibility, selection process, and official apply link. Always confirm dates on the government portal before applying.

AP DSC (District Selection Committee) / Teacher Recruitment Test (TRT) is conducted by the Department of School Education, Andhra Pradesh to fill teaching vacancies in government, Zilla Parishad and aided schools. AP DSC 2026 recruits School Assistants (SA), Secondary Grade Teachers (SGT), Language Pandits (LP), Physical Education Teachers (PET) and other teaching posts. Candidates must hold relevant educational and teacher training qualifications along with qualifying AP TET (Andhra Pradesh Teacher Eligibility Test) marks.

Eligibility

  • Nationality: Indian citizen.
  • Domicile: Andhra Pradesh local/non-local status as applicable.
  • Educational Qualification: B.Ed for SA posts; D.Ed for SGT; B.Ed with language specialization for LP; relevant degree for PET.
  • TET Requirement: Must have qualified AP TET / CTET with minimum 60% marks (55% for SC/ST/BC/PwBD).
  • Age Limit: 18–46 years as on 1st July of notification year. Relaxation for reserved categories as per AP rules.
  • Language Proficiency: Must pass Telugu Language Pandit test or possess Telugu as a subject at degree level where required.

Advertisement

Selection Process

  • Written Examination (CBT): General Knowledge, Current Affairs, Perspectives in Education, Content & Methodology (subject-specific) and Teaching Aptitude.
  • Weightage: TET marks carry weightage (typically 20%) in final merit along with DSC written exam marks (80%).
  • Certificate Verification: Original certificates of education, TET, caste and local status verified for shortlisted candidates.
  • Post allotment based on merit, category reservation and post preferences.
  • Probationary appointment in government schools across Andhra Pradesh districts.

How to Apply

  1. Visit dsc.ap.gov.in when AP DSC/TRT 2026 notification is published.
  2. Register on the portal with Aadhaar-linked mobile number and email address.
  3. Select the post category (SGT, SA, LP, PET etc.) and fill educational and TET details.
  4. Upload photograph, signature and supporting documents.
  5. Pay application fee online (fee varies by category — SC/ST often exempted or reduced).
  6. Submit application, download confirmation and hall ticket when exam schedule is announced.
