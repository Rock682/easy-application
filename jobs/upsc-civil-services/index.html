UPSC Civil Services Examination 2026

Verified guide — eligibility, selection process, and official apply link. Always confirm dates on the government portal before applying.

The UPSC Civil Services Examination is India's most prestigious competitive exam, selecting officers for the Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Police Service (IPS), Indian Foreign Service (IFS) and other Group A and B central services. UPSC Civil Services 2026 follows the three-stage process of Preliminary Examination, Main Examination and Personality Test (Interview). Graduates who aspire to serve in top leadership roles in the Union and state governments apply through upsc.gov.in when the annual notification is published, typically in February.

Eligibility

  • Nationality: Indian citizen for IAS and IPS; for other services — citizen of India, Nepal/Bhutan subject, Tibetan refugee, or Indian origin migrant from specified countries.
  • Educational Qualification: Bachelor's degree from a recognized university. Final-year students may apply but must produce proof before Main Examination.
  • Age Limit: 21–32 years on 1st August of exam year. Relaxation: OBC — 3 years; SC/ST — 5 years; PwBD — 10 years; Defence personnel — up to 5 years.
  • Number of Attempts: General — 6; OBC — 9; SC/ST — unlimited (till age limit).
  • Physical Standards: IPS and certain services require meeting specified physical fitness criteria.

Selection Process

  • Stage 1 — Preliminary Examination: Two papers (GS Paper I and CSAT Paper II). GS Paper I is merit-ranking; CSAT is qualifying (33% minimum).
  • Stage 2 — Main Examination: Nine descriptive papers including Essay, four GS papers, two optional subject papers and two language papers (qualifying).
  • Stage 3 — Personality Test (Interview): 275 marks assessing mental alertness, leadership, ethics and communication skills.
  • Final merit based on Main Examination (1750 marks) + Interview (275 marks) = 2025 marks total.
  • Service allocation (IAS, IPS, IFS, etc.) based on rank, category and candidate preferences.

How to Apply

  1. Visit upsc.gov.in and navigate to 'Active Examinations' when Civil Services notification is released.
  2. Click 'Apply Online' for Civil Services Examination and complete Part I (Registration) with basic details.
  3. Complete Part II — choose exam centre, optional subject (for Mains), upload photo, signature and ID proof.
  4. Pay examination fee of ₹100 (SC/ST/PwBD/female candidates exempted) through online payment.
  5. Submit the application and download the e-admission certificate / confirmation.
  6. Appear for Prelims as per schedule; qualified candidates must submit Detailed Application Form (DAF) for Mains.
