SSC CGL 2026 — Combined Graduate Level Recruitment

Verified guide — eligibility, selection process, and official apply link. Always confirm dates on the government portal before applying.

The Staff Selection Commission Combined Graduate Level (SSC CGL) examination is one of India's largest central government recruitment drives. SSC CGL 2026 fills thousands of Group B and Group C posts — including Assistant Section Officer, Inspector, Auditor, Tax Assistant and Statistical Investigator — across ministries, departments and attached offices of the Government of India. Candidates with a graduate degree can apply online when the official notification is released on ssc.gov.in.

Eligibility

  • Nationality: Must be a citizen of India, or subject of Nepal/Bhutan, or Tibetan refugee settled in India before 1962, or person of Indian origin migrated from specified countries with intent to permanently settle in India.
  • Educational Qualification: Bachelor's degree from a recognized university. Specific posts may require additional qualifications (e.g., Statistics, Economics, Accountancy).
  • Age Limit: 18–32 years as on the date specified in the notification. Age relaxation: OBC — 3 years; SC/ST — 5 years; PwBD — 10 years; Ex-servicemen as per rules.
  • Physical Standards: Applicable only for Inspector (Central Excise/Preventive Officer/Examiner) and Sub-Inspector in CBI/NIA posts.

Selection Process

  • Tier 1: Computer-Based Examination (CBT) — General Intelligence, General Awareness, Quantitative Aptitude and English Comprehension.
  • Tier 2: CBT covering Quantitative Abilities, English Language & Comprehension, Statistics (for JSO post) and General Studies (Finance & Economics for specific posts).
  • Tier 3: Descriptive Paper — Essay, Letter and Application writing in English or Hindi.
  • Tier 4: Skill Test / Document Verification — DEST (Data Entry Speed Test) or CPT (Computer Proficiency Test) where applicable, followed by document verification.
  • Final merit is prepared based on normalized scores across tiers and post preferences submitted during application.

How to Apply

  1. Visit the official SSC website at ssc.gov.in and click on the active CGL 2026 notification link.
  2. Register with a valid email ID and mobile number to obtain your Registration Number and Password.
  3. Fill the online application form with personal, educational and category details; upload photograph and signature as per specifications.
  4. Pay the application fee online (₹100 for General/OBC; fee exempted for SC/ST/PwBD/Ex-servicemen and all women candidates).
  5. Submit the form, download and print the confirmation page for future reference.
  6. Keep checking ssc.gov.in for admit card, exam city intimation and result updates.
